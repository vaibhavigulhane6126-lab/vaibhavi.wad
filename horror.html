HORROR MOVIE

Setting: Often isolated or eerie places—abandoned houses, dark forests, haunted hospitals, or small towns with secrets.
Atmosphere: Heavy use of shadows, unsettling silence, sudden loud sounds, and chilling music to build tension.
Characters: A group of unsuspecting people (friends, family, or strangers).
A central antagonist (ghost, demon, killer, monster, or even a psychological threat).
Survivors who must confront their fears and fight back.
Plot Elements: Strange occurrences that escalate into terrifying events.
Secrets revealed about the antagonist or cursed location.
A fight for survival, often with unexpected twists.
Themes: Fear of the unknown, mortality, isolation, revenge, or supernatural forces.
Ending: Frequently ambiguous—evil may not be fully defeated, leaving viewers unsettled.

Classic & Influential Horror Films
Nosferatu (1922) – German expressionist vampire tale.
Psycho (1960) – Alfred Hitchcock’s psychological thriller.
The Exorcist (1973) – Groundbreaking supernatural horror.
Halloween (1978) – Defined the slasher genre.
The Shining (1980) – Stanley Kubrick’s unsettling masterpiece.
A Nightmare on Elm Street (1984) – Introduced Freddy Krueger.
The Silence of the Lambs (1991) – Psychological horror with Hannibal Lecter.

Modern Horror Favorites
Scream (1996) – Meta-slasher revitalizing the genre.
The Ring (2002) – Japanese-inspired supernatural horror.
Paranormal Activity (2007) – Found-footage phenomenon.
The Conjuring (2013) – Based on real paranormal investigations.
